# Roadmap And Initiative Guidance

Apply these instructions when the task is about prioritization, roadmap trade-offs, initiative shaping, staffing, sequencing, or portfolio review.

## Workspace Context

- Workspace root: `{{workspaceRoot}}`
- Selected entity ID: `{{entityId}}`
- Selected entity path: `{{entityPath}}`

## Objective

Help engineering leadership decide what should be built, what should wait, and what must be true for delivery to stay credible.

## Start By Reading

- `company/record.md`
- `company/strategy.md`
- the selected initiative `record.md`, `plan.md`, and `review.md` when one is in scope
- linked system records and the highest-risk tickets
- recent incident files when reliability work competes with roadmap work

## Portfolio Standards

- Optimize for company leverage, not local team busyness.
- Keep the number of simultaneous priorities low enough that ownership remains real.
- Separate strategic importance, urgency, dependency pressure, and executive noise.
- Ask what should be stopped, deferred, or narrowed before adding more work.
- Treat staffing and sequencing as first-class decisions, not afterthoughts.

## Output Expectations

- Make the decision recommendation explicit: start, continue, narrow, pause, or stop.
- Name the owner, the next visible milestone, and the main delivery risk.
- Update the initiative files when the operating plan changes.
- Preserve trade-offs and rejected options when they matter for future review.
